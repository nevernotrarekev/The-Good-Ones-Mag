Name - Issue - Volume 1 - Spring 2012
First off, how are you doing? What have you been up to?

I’m doing well and things with Dirty Richmond are too. I’m at a place with Dirty Richmond where many of the goals that I originally had set have been achieved and I just reached over 5,000 tumblr followers a few weeks ago, so now I feel as if I’m on a victory lap. Blogging has always came naturally too me and now that I’m in a solid routine, it’s even easier. I still stay up late working on things from time to time. though but not nearly as often as I used to.

You’re known around your city for being behind Dirty Richmond. Can you describe that project?

Dirty Richmond originally began as a collection of street style photographs that started to take and post during the late summer of 2009. This eventually evolved beyond just street style to include more of the lifestyle and culture within the city.

What inspired you to start it?

Before starting Dirty Richmond I closely followed Scott Schuman’s The Sartorialist for years. I’ve always been interested in not only what people were wearing but also about the people themselves and thought Richmond would be the perfect place to have a blog like Dirty Richmond based.

Why do you say Richmond would be a perfect place to do something like that?

There’s an independent spirit that exist here that doesn’t exist in most college cities and towns. When I studied abroad in Hong Kong I felt different and a bit out of place from most of the other American students that were there. When people saw me around they thought I was British or something likek that just based on the way I was dressing. But here in Richmond, I’ve never had that feeling of being out of place. People have the freedom to do their own thing here and that’s why this is such a great place photographing such independent diversity on a consistent basis.

I. AMERICAN APPAREL Button-Up

This flannel shirt from American Apparel is one of the best shirts I’ve ever purchased and people are always interested in the material. It’s soft but has a lot of heft to it. Levi 511’s are the only jeans I’ve purchased for years and I don’t know how many black ones I’ve bought since my first. Eventually, I’d like to buy a pair of selvedge denim and wear them forever but I wear blue/indigo denim so infrequently that I feel odd in them.
